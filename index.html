Project Overview

Investigating correlations between land surface dynamics and atmospheric trends across the Netherlands.

Objectives

  • Detect land cover transitions between 2021 and 2023 using ESRI 10m data
  • Correlate changes with PM₂.₅, NO₂, and PM₁₀ trends from CAMS reanalysis
  • Assess population exposure across land use zones at GAUL L2 level
  • Build an interactive WebGIS to visualize results

Study Area

Netherlands — a densely populated country in Western Europe, characterized by intensive agriculture, urban development, and significant land use dynamics. Assigned as Group 2 case study for the GIS Laboratory project.

Data Sources

  • CAMS European Air Quality Reanalysis (0.1°)
  • ESRI 10m Annual Land Cover (2017-2024)
  • WorldPop Population Counts (100m, 2023)
  • GAUL Administrative Boundaries (Level 2)

Processing Workflow

1

Pollutant Mapping

NetCDF to monthly aggregation, annual averages, EU concentration classification, and change detection (2023-2021).

↓
2

Land Cover Change

ESRI tile reprojection to WGS84, VRT merge, boundary clip, and raster calculation (LC₂₀₂₁ × 100 + LC₂₀₂₃).

↓
3

Correlation & Exposure

Binary change masks, zonal statistics, population overlay at GAUL L2, and bivariate mapping.

↓
4

WebGIS & Website

OpenLayers interactive map with AMAC overlay, basemap switching, and group member results.
